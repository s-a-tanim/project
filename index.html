Project 1

Prediction of Vehicle CO2 Emissions with eXplainable AI Integration for Environmental Sustainability

Brief Description

In response to the growing environmental concerns associated with CO2 emissions from transportation. This project developed CarbonMLP, a deep learning model designed to predict vehicle CO2 emissions based on various vehicle attributes such as engine type, fuel efficiency, weight, and other specifications. The model not only provides accurate predictions but also integrates explainable AI (XAI) techniques to enhance transparency, interpretability. This approach make it easier to understand the driving factors behind CO2 emissions. This project aims to support environmental sustainability by providing a powerful tool to identify and mitigate high CO2 emissions from vehicles.

Relevant Background

Transportation is one of the largest contributors to greenhouse gas emissions, primarily through the emission of carbon dioxide (CO2). CO2 is a major driver of global warming, resulting in an increase in average global temperatures and contributing to extreme weather events such as floods, heatwaves, rising sea levels, and more. Vehicle emissions play a significant role in this, with high-performance vehicles often producing more CO2 than fuel-efficient ones.

By accurately predicting vehicle CO2 emissions, it becomes possible to:

  • Track pollution sources: Understanding which vehicle models contribute the most CO2 allows for more targeted interventions and regulations.
  • Encourage green vehicle adoption: Governments and consumers can identify fuel-efficient vehicles, thus promoting cleaner alternatives.
  • Optimize vehicle usage: For businesses managing fleets, accurate CO2 predictions help in planning for more eco-friendly fleet management.

This project utilizes a dataset from the Canadian government's open data to analyze the CO2 emissions of different vehicles and use deep learning techniques to predict their emissions. The approach integrates both machine learning and deep learning models to provide robust predictions that can drive actionable insights for environmental policies, regulations, and consumer decisions.

Sustainability Impacts and CO2 Emission Analysis

In the context of sustainability, this project goes beyond just predicting CO2 emissions. It provides actionable data that can help shape a greener future. Here's how the project contributes to sustainability:

  • Promoting Clean Energy Use: By analyzing the relationship between vehicle attributes (e.g., engine type, fuel consumption, weight) and CO2 emissions. The project provides data to help consumers make eco-friendly decisions when purchasing a vehicle. Fuel-efficient vehicles, or electric vehicles (EVs), emit significantly less CO2, thus helping to reduce the overall carbon footprint.
  • Policy Design and Regulation: Governments can use these predictions to design carbon taxation policies or emission standards for vehicles. With accurate prediction tools, governments can identify high-emission vehicles and enforce stricter regulations for these vehicles, promoting the transition to more sustainable transportation.
  • Carbon Footprint Reduction: For organizations managing fleets, being able to predict CO2 emissions allows for more strategic planning. Fleet managers can replace old, high-emission vehicles with energy-efficient or electric vehicles, reducing their fleet's overall carbon footprint. This has direct implications for improving a company's sustainability credentials.
  • Environmental Impact Forecasting: The model enables long-term forecasting by simulating various scenarios where different kinds of vehicles are introduced into the market. The results from these simulations can inform future decisions on how to reduce overall transportation emissions. For example, governments can forecast the impact of transitioning from conventional fuel cars to electric vehicles (EVs) on national CO2 levels.

    • Python, TensorFlow, Keras for deep learning implementation
    • SHAP (Shapley Additive Explanations) for model interpretability

  • Methodologies:

    • Statistical analysis for feature importance and CO2 emission forecasting

Additionally, I contributed to building the project proposal and conducted the analytical work to evaluate the sustainability impact of our model's predictions by ensuring the results align with long-term environmental goals.

Project 3

Secure Federated Learning for Parkinson's Disease: Non-IID Data Partitioning and Homomorphic Encryption Strategies

Brief Description:
In this project, we explore methods to improve both privacyterms of federated learning (FL) models by implementing two key techniques: homomorphic encryption and attention-based fusion. In FL it involves client-side training of models using multiple users' data which suffers from drawbacks such as privacy issues and the variability of data across different users (data heterogeneity). To address these challenges, we introduced a Secure Federated Learning (SFL) algorithm that avoids problems associated with Non-Independent and Identically Distributed (Non-IID) data by introducing a proposed partitioning method for datasets. Then, we used homomorphic encryption to securely sum encrypted model parameters. Additionally, attention-based fusion techniques were applied to boost model accuracy and robustness.

Relevant Background:
Parkinson's disease (PD) is a progressive neurological disorder thats why detecting its progression early can significantly impact treatment for patients. FL offers a privacy-preserving framework for training machine learning models on distributed data, but the challenge arises from the variability in data (heterogeneity) and the need to protect sensitive patient data. In this study, we handle the problem of Non-IID data in federated learning using homomorphic encryption and attention-based fusion techniques by demonstrating the potential for scalable and secure learning in healthcare.

Sustainability Impacts and Data Security:
This project addresses two major concerns: improving model accuracy while preserving privacy. The use of homomorphic encryption ensures that sensitive health data remains secure, while attention-based fusion improves model robustness, leading to better disease progression predictions. This approach can be scaled to other healthcare applications where data security is a priority, promoting both privacy and sustainability in data usage.

Skills Required:

  • Technical Skills: Python, PySyft, PyTorch, Homomorphic Encryption, Federated Learning Techniques
  • Methodologies: Federated Learning, Homomorphic Encryption, Attention-Based Fusion, Secure Model Aggregation

My Contribution:
Involving developed the Secure Federated Learning algorithm to address the challenges of Non-IID data concepts and implemented the homomorphic encryption strategy for secure model training. Furthermore, I evaluated the model's performance and analyzed the results, focusing on ensuring privacy and efficiency in clinical applications.

Achievements/Results:

  • Significant improvement in robustness compared to traditional methods.
  • Successful encryption and summation of model parameters without compromising performance.
  • Validation of the model's reliability and applicability in clinical settings.
  • Demonstrated the feasibility of federated learning in sensitive data environments particularly in healthcare.

Tools/Technologies Used:

  • Python, PyTorch for deep learning
  • PySyft for federated learning
  • Homomorphic Encryption libraries for secure data processing
  • Matplotlib, Seaborn for data visualization

Project 4

Prediction and Management of Urban Heat Islands Using Deep Learning and Remote Sensing

Brief Description:
This project addresses the growing challenge of urban heat islands (UHIs) in metropolitan areas by developing a deep learning framework that combines satellite imagery, meteorological data, and urban infrastructure information. The model predicts temperature variations across urban landscapes with high spatial resolution, helping city planners identify hot spots and implement targeted mitigation strategies. Our approach integrates computer vision techniques with climatological modeling to create a comprehensive heat management solution for sustainable urban development.

Relevant Background:
Urban Heat Islands are metropolitan areas that experience significantly higher temperatures than surrounding rural regions due to human activities and urban development. The UHI effect can increase energy consumption, elevate pollution levels, compromise water quality, and threaten public health, particularly for vulnerable populations during extreme heat events. As urbanization continues globally, the severity and frequency of UHIs are expected to increase, making this a critical area for research and intervention in sustainable urban planning.

Sustainability Impacts:
This project directly addresses UN Sustainable Development Goals by providing tools for climate action and sustainable cities. By accurately predicting UHI patterns, our model enables:

  • Energy Conservation: Optimizing cooling strategies during peak heat periods
  • Green Space Planning: Strategic placement of parks and vegetation to maximize cooling effects
  • Vulnerable Population Protection: Identifying high-risk areas for targeted intervention during heat waves
  • Climate Resilience: Developing long-term urban design strategies that mitigate the UHI effect

Skills Required:

  • Technical Skills: Python, TensorFlow, Keras, GIS software, Remote sensing data processing
  • Methodologies: Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), U-Net architecture for image segmentation, Time series analysis, Geospatial modeling

My Contribution:
Led the development of the deep learning architecture, focusing on creating a model that could effectively integrate multispectral satellite imagery with urban infrastructure data. Designed and implemented the data preprocessing pipeline for handling diverse data sources. Conducted validation studies comparing model predictions with ground-based temperature measurements from weather stations across three major cities, ensuring the model's applicability across different urban contexts.

Achievements/Results:

  • Achieved temperature prediction accuracy within ±1.2°C of actual measurements
  • Successfully identified microclimate variations at 10-meter spatial resolution
  • Developed an interactive visualization tool for city planners to simulate the impact of various green infrastructure interventions
  • Model predictions led to optimized placement of urban cooling centers in a pilot implementation program

Tools/Technologies Used:

  • Deep Learning: TensorFlow, Keras for model development
  • Remote Sensing: Landsat, Sentinel satellite imagery processing
  • GIS Software: QGIS, ArcGIS for spatial analysis
  • Data Integration: Google Earth Engine for large-scale data processing

Project 5

AquaSense: IoT-Based Water Quality Monitoring with Blockchain Integration for Data Integrity

Brief Description:
AquaSense is an integrated IoT and blockchain solution designed to monitor, analyze, and secure water quality data from freshwater ecosystems. The system employs distributed sensor networks to collect real-time measurements of key water quality parameters including pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, temperature, and the presence of specific contaminants. Data is transmitted to a cloud platform where advanced machine learning algorithms detect anomalies and predict potential water quality deterioration. To ensure data integrity and transparency, the system implements a permissioned blockchain architecture that creates an immutable record of all measurements, making the data trustworthy for regulatory compliance and environmental research.

Relevant Background:
Access to clean water is a fundamental human right and essential for ecosystem health, yet water sources worldwide face increasing threats from pollution, climate change, and industrial activities. Traditional water quality monitoring is often labor-intensive, intermittent, and susceptible to data manipulation or errors. This project addresses these challenges by creating a continuous, tamper-proof monitoring system that can detect contamination events in near real-time, while maintaining an auditable record of all water quality data.

Sustainability Impacts:
AquaSense contributes to environmental sustainability through:

  • Early Contamination Detection: Real-time monitoring enables rapid response to pollution events, minimizing ecological damage
  • Resource Optimization: Targeted water treatment based on actual conditions rather than predetermined schedules
  • Long-term Ecosystem Analysis: Creating comprehensive datasets for understanding aquatic ecosystem dynamics
  • Regulatory Compliance: Providing immutable evidence for environmental regulations enforcement
  • Community Empowerment: Increasing transparency by making water quality data publicly accessible

Skills Required:

  • Technical Skills: IoT sensor programming, Embedded systems development, Blockchain implementation (Hyperledger Fabric), Cloud architecture, Machine learning for anomaly detection
  • Methodologies: Time series analysis, LSTM networks for predictive modeling, Consensus mechanisms for blockchain validation

My Contribution:
Designed the blockchain architecture for data integrity verification and developed the smart contracts governing data validation and access control. Created the anomaly detection algorithms that process incoming sensor data to identify abnormal water quality patterns. Coordinated field testing with environmental agencies to validate system performance under various conditions and optimized the energy efficiency of the sensor nodes to enable long-term deployment in remote locations.

Achievements/Results:

  • Successfully deployed a network of 50+ sensor nodes across three watershed areas
  • Achieved 94% accuracy in detecting contamination events with a false positive rate below 3%
  • Reduced anomaly detection latency to under 15 minutes from sensor measurement
  • Implemented a tamper-proof blockchain record with over 10 million data points securely stored
  • Developed a public-facing dashboard with over 2,000 monthly active users from environmental agencies and research institutions

Tools/Technologies Used:

  • Hardware: Arduino-based custom sensor arrays, LoRaWAN for long-range data transmission
  • Blockchain: Hyperledger Fabric for permissioned blockchain implementation
  • Cloud: AWS IoT Core for device management, Lambda for serverless processing
  • Machine Learning: TensorFlow for anomaly detection and water quality prediction
  • Visualization: Grafana for real-time dashboards and alerts

Note: AquaSense has been selected for extended deployment by the Environmental Protection Agency as part of their next-generation water monitoring initiative. A detailed technical white paper on the system architecture and implementation is available upon request.
